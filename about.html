About

husk scheme is developed by Justin Ethier.

The original goal of the husk project was to teach myself Scheme, Haskell, and functional programming by going though Jonathan Tang's excellent tutorial, Write Yourself a Scheme in 48 Hours. Ultimately this project went far beyond the content in Jonathan's tutorial, and implemented a (mostly) fully-functional Scheme interpreter and macro subsystem. At its core, husk is still a learning project, although my hope is that over time it may become more than just a toy Scheme. On the one hand it has the potential to be useful for real-world applications by providing R<5>RS compatibility and an FFI to Haskell libraries. But on the other, Haskell is a great language in its own right, and husk is unlikely to approach the speed of native C implementations such as Chicken. That said, I hope people will be interested to experiment and play around with husk as it continues to improve.

Patches are welcome and may be sent by pull request on github. When making code changes please try to add at least one test case for your change, and ensure that the change does not break any existing unit tests.
